How it works

Every time we generate a token, we compute its Key and Value. We then save them to the Cache Bank.

For the next token, we don't re-calculate Token 1's K and V. We just fetch them from the bank.

Why Not Cache Q? (The Math)

\(\text{Out}_t = \text{softmax}(Q_t \cdot K_{\le t}^T) \cdot V_{\le t}\)

Look at the formulation for generating step \(t\). It only requires \(Q_t\). The previous queries (\(Q_1 \dots Q_{t-1}\)) were consumed to generate previous words and are mathematically never referenced again. Storing them would just waste RAM!

Attention Complexity: \(O(n^2)\) → \(O(n)\) per token
